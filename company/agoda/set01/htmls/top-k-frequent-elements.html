You may return the answer in **any order**. **Example 1:** **Input:**nums = [1,1,1,2,2,3], k = 2 **Output:**[1,2] **Example 2:** **Input:**nums = [1], k = 1 **Output:**[1] **Example 3:** **Input:**nums = [1,2,1,2,1,2,3,1,3,2], k = 2 **Output:**[1,2] **Constraints:** * `1 <= nums.length <= 105` * `-104 <= nums[i] <= 104` * `k` is in the range `[1, the number of unique elements in the array]`. * It is **guaranteed** that the answer is **unique**. **Follow up:** Your algorithm's time complexity must be better than `O(n log n)`, where n is the array's size.
